The Shapes

This collection is created from fabrics, which are cut in various shapes like L-shape, rectangle and oval. Unlike the traditional method of pattern making, these are created by draping and turning them inside out during the process, which creates an interesting silhouette. Most of the garments have minimal seams, finished by innovative methods of sewing.

Phi: Golden Ratio

This collection is inspired by Phi, the golden ratio found in nature, also known as a Fibonacci spiral. It can result in a nesting process which is repeated into infinity. It surrounds us from a tiny snail to the massive galaxies in our universe. The silhouette of this collection recreates the infinite spiral with luxurious fabrics and colors.

The Shapes II

A sequel to "The Shapes" collection, jackets and pants further explore the concept of fabrics cut in basic shapes to create these garments. Fluidity and volume are the key visual elements. Created with woolen fabrics and a hint of crimson floral print are incorporated to enhance the aesthetic appeal and create an element of surprise.

Research

Researching and collecting information about silhouettes and fabrics is the initial steps towards creating any collection. Rough sketches and initial ideas are jotted down for inspiration as we proceed.


Explore

Exploring with fabrics to understand their nature and flow makes an important step towards creating silhouettes which are voluminous and justify the nature of the collection. Miniature garments were handcrafted for experimentation before the muslin fits to save time and fabric.


Document

Flat sketching gives a better idea of the patterns used to create a garment, it includes various details like the fabric used, seams incorpoarted, trims and thier placements.
